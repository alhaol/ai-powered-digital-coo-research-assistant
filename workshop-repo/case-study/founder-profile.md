# Founder Profile: Ahmad Saad & Kinsight

> ⚠️ Fictional founder, fictional company, synthetic data. Written for teaching only.

## Meet the Founder

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Name** | Ahmad Saad |
| **Background** | Healthcare data engineer, 10 years building EHR integrations for a hospital network |
| **Startup** | **Kinsight** — a platform connecting parents and clinicians around a young person's mental health |
| **Stage** | Pre-revenue, pre-product. Has savings, a working prototype, and a personal reason. |
| **Problem He Sees** | Parents see everything and can record none of it. Clinicians see a 50-minute slice every six weeks and rebuild the history from memory. Nothing connects the two. |
| **His Constraints** | Solo founder. $8,000 bootstrap budget. No clinical team. No data analyst. No COO. |

## His Starting Point (Monday morning)

Ahmad's daughter waited five months for an appointment. In that time he kept notes on
his phone that nobody ever read. He has a vague idea — *"an app that tells parents how
their teen is doing"* — but no structured hypothesis, no data, and no operational system.
What he does have:

- 10 years of health-data plumbing and a prototype that ingests parent check-ins
- A hunch that the gap is **between** appointments, not inside them
- $8,000 and roughly one week before he decides whether to leave his job

## Why This Case Study Threads the Whole Workshop

Ahmad's week models exactly what participants do in each module. As he moves through
Build → Measure → Learn → Operate, he also builds his `.claude/` toolkit — graduating
from *using* prompts to *building* reusable commands and, finally, a bundled skill.

| Day | Module | What Ahmad produces | Command / Skill he builds |
|-----|--------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Mon | BUILD | Hypotheses, interview script, landing page brief | `/hypothesis` |
| Wed | MEASURE | Competitive analysis, survey report, insights | `/competitive-analysis`, `/analyze-survey` |
| Thu | LEARN | Pivot decision, sprint plan | `/pivot-decision`, `/sprint-plan` |
| Fri | OPERATE | SOPs, checklists, dashboard | `/generate-sop` + `lean-startup` skill |

## The Tension (the moment that makes the story)

Ahmad's original bet is an **AI risk score** — a number telling parents whether their
teen is struggling. It's the instinct of a man who has spent a decade making systems
measurable: if you can't measure it, you can't fix it.

His data says **76% of parents would not accept an algorithmic score about their own
child.** What they want — 64% of them — is to know *what to say tonight*. Meanwhile 85%
of clinicians name intake and documentation as their worst pain, and 70% would pay for
help with it.

Module 3 is where he decides: pivot, persevere, or zoom in? The data — not his
engineering instinct — makes the call.

> **The lesson, twice over.** The Lean one: Ahmad doesn't use AI to *think* for him, he
> uses it to *move* faster. The judgment is his; the grunt work is the AI's.
>
> And the one this domain adds: **the data talked him out of building a diagnostic.**
> The riskiest feature was also the one his users didn't want and couldn't trust. That
> is not a coincidence — in health, "nobody wants this" and "you shouldn't ship this"
> tend to arrive together. Listening to customers and behaving responsibly pointed the
> same direction.
