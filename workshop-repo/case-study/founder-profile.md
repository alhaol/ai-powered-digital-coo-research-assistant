# Founder Profile: Priya Sharma & GlowEarth

## Meet the Founder

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Name** | Priya Sharma |
| **Background** | Former cosmetics chemist, 8 years in the beauty industry |
| **Startup** | **GlowEarth** — a DTC sustainable skincare brand |
| **Stage** | Pre-revenue, pre-product. Has savings, a formulation notebook, and a burning idea. |
| **Problem She Sees** | Greenwashing is rampant in skincare. Consumers want sustainable products but can't verify claims. |
| **Her Constraints** | Solo founder. $3,000 bootstrap budget. No marketing team. No data analyst. No COO. |

## Her Starting Point (Monday morning)

Priya has a vague idea — *"sustainable skincare for millennials"* — but no
structured hypothesis, no data, and no operational system. What she does have:

- 8 years of chemistry expertise and a notebook of formulations
- A hunch that **transparency**, not just "clean" labels, is the real unmet need
- $3,000 and roughly one week before she has to decide whether to commit

## Why This Case Study Threads the Whole Workshop

Priya's week models exactly what participants do in each module. As she moves
through Build → Measure → Learn → Operate, she also builds her `.claude/`
toolkit — graduating from *using* prompts to *building* reusable commands and,
finally, a bundled skill.

| Day | Module | What Priya produces | Command / Skill she builds |
|-----|--------|---------------------|----------------------------|
| Mon | BUILD | Hypotheses, interview script, landing page brief | `/project:hypothesis` |
| Wed | MEASURE | Competitive analysis, survey report, insights | `/project:competitive-analysis`, `/project:analyze-survey` |
| Thu | LEARN | Pivot decision, sprint plan | `/project:pivot-decision`, `/project:sprint-plan` |
| Fri | OPERATE | SOPs, checklists, dashboard | `/project:generate-sop` + `lean-startup` skill |

## The Tension (the moment that makes the story)

Priya's original bet is **carbon-neutral shipping** as the hero feature. Her
survey data says customers care far more about **ingredient transparency**.
Module 3 is where she has to decide: pivot, persevere, or zoom in? The data — not
her ego — makes the call.

> **The lesson:** Priya doesn't use AI to *think* for her. She uses it to *move*
> faster. The judgment is hers. The grunt work is the AI's.
