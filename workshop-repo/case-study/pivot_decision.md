## Hypothesis Under Review
"Eco-conscious millennials aged 25-35 will pay a **20% premium** for skincare with
verified **carbon-neutral shipping** and refillable packaging."

## Evidence Summary
| Source | Key Finding | Supports Hypothesis? |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Survey (n=50) | 72% rank **ingredient transparency** as their #1 concern | ❌ |
| Survey (n=50) | Only **28%** put carbon-neutral shipping in their top 3 | ❌ |
| Survey (n=50) | Only **22%** will pay a 20% premium; **60%** will pay 10-15% | ⚠️ |
| Survey (n=50) | Most requested feature: **QR code** linking to ingredient sourcing | ✅ (new direction) |
| Competitive scan | **3/5** competitors already claim carbon-neutral shipping | ❌ (not differentiating) |
| Competitive scan | **0/5** offer verifiable per-ingredient traceability | ✅ (white space) |

## Recommendation
**Decision: ZOOM IN**

The broad thesis holds — this segment *will* pay a premium for **proven**
sustainability. But the specific lever is wrong. Carbon-neutral shipping is table
stakes and ranks low with customers. Ingredient traceability is simultaneously
the top customer concern and an uncontested market gap.

## Revised Hypothesis
> "We believe eco-conscious millennials aged 25-35 have no trustworthy way to
> verify skincare ingredient claims and will pay a **10-15% premium** if we offer
> **full ingredient traceability via QR codes.**"

## Risk Assessment
The biggest risk is **stated preference ≠ purchase behavior**. 72% *say*
transparency matters most; we haven't watched them pay for it. **Leading
indicator to watch:** pre-order conversion on the QR-traceability landing page.
If conversion is < 3% after $200 of ad spend, transparency is a "nice to have,"
not a buying trigger — and we test the refill angle next.

## Confidence Level: HIGH
Two independent signals agree (customer survey + competitive gap), and the
revised hypothesis *reduces* risk by lowering the price premium from an
unsupported 20% to a validated 10-15%.
