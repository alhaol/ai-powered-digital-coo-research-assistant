# Pivot-or-Persevere Analysis — GlowEarth

## Hypothesis Under Review
"We believe eco-conscious millennials aged 25-35 will pay a 20% premium for
skincare with verified carbon-neutral shipping and refillable packaging."

## Evidence Summary
| Source | Key Finding | Supports Hypothesis? |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Survey (n=50) | 72% rank ingredient transparency #1; only 28% rank carbon-neutral shipping top-3 | ❌ |
| Survey (n=50) | Only 22% will pay a 20% premium; 60% will pay 10-15% | ⚠️ |
| Competitive scan (5 brands) | 3/5 already claim carbon-neutral shipping; 0/5 offer full ingredient traceability | ❌ (lever), ✅ (gap) |

## Recommendation
**Decision:** ZOOM IN

The broad thesis — eco-conscious millennials will pay a premium for *proven*
sustainability — holds. The specific lever is wrong. Carbon-neutral shipping is
table stakes (3/5 competitors claim it) and ranks low for customers. Ingredient
traceability is both the top-ranked customer concern and a clear white space.

## Revised Hypothesis
"We believe eco-conscious millennials aged 25-35 have no trustworthy way to
verify skincare ingredient claims and will pay a 10-15% premium if we offer full
ingredient traceability via QR codes."

## Risk Assessment
Stated preference ≠ purchase behavior. 72% *say* transparency matters most, but
we haven't seen them pay for it. Leading indicator to watch: pre-order
conversion rate on the QR-traceability landing page. If < 3% after $200 of ad
spend, transparency is a "nice to have," not a buying trigger.

## Confidence Level: High
Two independent signals agree (customer survey + competitive gap), and the
revised hypothesis lowers the price-premium risk from 20% to a validated 10-15%.
