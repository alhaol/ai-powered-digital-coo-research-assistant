## 3 Testable Hypotheses — GlowEarth

### 1. Ingredient Transparency
> "We believe **eco-conscious millennials (25-35)** have **no trustworthy way to
> verify skincare sustainability claims** and will **pay a 10-15% premium** if we
> offer **full ingredient traceability via a QR code on every product.**"

- **Riskiest Assumption:** Stated preference for transparency converts to actual
  purchase.
- **Cheapest Test:** $200 pre-order landing page with QR-traceability as the hero.
- **Success Signal:** ≥ 50 pre-orders or ≥ 200 email signups in 14 days.

### 2. Refillable-First System
> "We believe **eco-conscious millennials** feel **guilty about packaging waste**
> and will **switch to a refill-by-default system** if we offer **refills cheaper
> than new bottles on a subscription.**"

- **Riskiest Assumption:** Refill logistics stay cheap enough to protect margin.
- **Cheapest Test:** Waitlist landing page framing refills as the core product.
- **Success Signal:** ≥ 15% of visitors join the refill waitlist.

### 3. Carbon-Neutral Premium
> "We believe **eco-conscious millennials** want **lower-carbon skincare** and
> will **pay a 20% premium** if we offer **verified carbon-neutral shipping and
> production.**"

- **Riskiest Assumption:** Shipping is a differentiator, not table stakes.
- **Cheapest Test:** A/B ad test: "carbon-neutral" vs. "traceable ingredients."
- **Success Signal:** Carbon-neutral ad beats traceability ad on click-through.

---

> **Advisor note:** Hypothesis #1 has the strongest differentiation (no competitor
> owns it) and the lowest price risk. Test it first.
