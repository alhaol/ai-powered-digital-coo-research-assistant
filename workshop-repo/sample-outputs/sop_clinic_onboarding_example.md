# SOP-001: Onboarding a New Clinic

**Version:** 1.0 · **Owner:** Ahmad Saad · **Review:** quarterly

## 1. Purpose and Scope

**Purpose.** Take a clinic from "signed" to "first intake summary delivered" in under 10
business days, with no founder improvisation.

**In scope:** solo practices and group clinics (2–15 clinicians) licensing Kinsight
per seat.
**Out of scope:** school districts (different procurement, see SOP-004), anything
touching clinical safety or safeguarding (**not covered here — see §6**).

## 2. Roles
| Role | Who | Responsibility |
|------|-----|----------------|
| Onboarding lead | Ahmad | Every step below until there's a team |
| Clinic champion | Named clinician at the clinic | Internal advocate; first to receive a summary |
| Practice admin | Clinic staff | Seat assignment, parent invitations |

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Day 0 — Kickoff
1. Send the **Intro Email** (§5.1) within 24h of signature.
2. Book a 30-minute kickoff with the clinic champion.
3. Confirm: number of seats, EHR in use, and who invites parents.

### Day 1–2 — Setup
4. Create the clinic workspace; assign seats to named clinicians.
5. **Decision point — practice size:**
   - **If solo practice (1 clinician):** the clinician is their own admin. Skip to step 7.
   - **If group clinic (2–15):** identify a practice admin first. Do not assign seats
     until an admin is named — unowned seats never activate.
6. Walk the admin through inviting their first 3 families.
7. Send the **Setup Confirmation Email** (§5.2).

### Day 3–7 — First Summary
8. Confirm ≥ 3 families have completed at least one check-in.
9. **Decision point — parent engagement:**
   - **If ≥ 3 families checked in:** generate the first intake summary; deliver to the
     champion 24h before their next session.
   - **If < 3 families after 5 days:** the invitation is the bottleneck, not the product.
     Call the admin. Do not send a summary built on one family's data.
10. Collect champion feedback on summary #1 within 48h. **This is the moment the account
    is won or lost** — a clinician who edits summary #1 and keeps it will renew.

### Day 8–10 — Handoff
11. Send the **First-Week Check-In Email** (§5.3).
12. Log the account in the pipeline with a 30-day review date.

## 4. Escalation Criteria

Escalate to Ahmad immediately if:
- A clinician reports a summary that is **factually wrong** about a family → pull the
  summary, don't patch it.
- Any clinician asks whether Kinsight assesses, screens, or scores → answer: **it does
  not.** See §6.
- A clinic requests EHR write-access → out of scope for v1; do not promise it.
- **Anything in a parent check-in suggests a young person is at risk** → this SOP does
  not cover it. Stop and follow §6.

## 5. Email Templates

### 5.1 Intro
> **Subject:** Kinsight — getting you set up
>
> Hi [NAME], welcome aboard. I'm Ahmad, and for now I'm the whole company, so you'll
> be dealing with me directly.
>
> Next step is a 30-minute call to set up your seats and get your first families
> invited. Here's my calendar: [LINK].
>
> Before we talk, one thing worth saying plainly: Kinsight organizes what parents tell
> you between sessions. It doesn't assess, score, or flag anything. You stay the
> clinician — we just stop you doing archaeology at the start of every appointment.

### 5.2 Setup Confirmation
> **Subject:** You're set up — here's what happens next
>
> Hi [NAME], your workspace is live with [N] seats.
>
> [ADMIN_NAME] can invite families from the Families tab. Start with three — you'll get
> your first intake summary within a week, 24 hours before that family's next session.
>
> The summary arrives as a **draft you edit**. Nothing is saved to your records until
> you say so.

### 5.3 First-Week Check-In
> **Subject:** Did summary #1 save you any time?
>
> Hi [NAME] — you should have received your first intake summary this week.
>
> One question, and I want the honest answer: did it save you time, or did you rewrite
> it from scratch? If it was the second one, tell me what was wrong and I'll fix it
> this week.

## 6. What This SOP Does Not Cover

**Safeguarding and crisis escalation are out of scope for this document, deliberately.**

If a parent's check-in contains a disclosure of self-harm or immediate risk, that is
governed by **SOP-CLIN-001**, which is written with a qualified clinician and legal
counsel against the duty-of-care rules of the operating jurisdiction. It is not drafted
by a founder, and it is not drafted by an AI.

> This section exists so nobody reaches for this document in the moment that matters and
> finds it silent. An operational SOP saying "not here, go there" is safer than one that
> improvises.

## 7. Metrics
| Metric | Target |
|--------|-------:|
| Time to first intake summary | < 10 business days |
| Seats activated / seats sold | > 80% by day 14 |
| Summary #1 kept (not rewritten) | > 60% |
| 30-day clinic retention | > 90% |
