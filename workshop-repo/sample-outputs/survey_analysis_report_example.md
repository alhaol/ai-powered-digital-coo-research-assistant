- **Geography:** US urban/eco-leaning metros (Portland, Austin, Brooklyn, Seattle…)

### 2. Top 3 Pain Points (ranked by frequency)

|------|---------|------------------|

> Carbon-neutral shipping appears in *any* top-3 for only **28%** of respondents.

### 3. Willingness-to-Pay Premium
| Premium tolerance | % of respondents |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Will pay **20%+** | **22%** (11/50) |

| List price only (0%) | 18% (9/50) |

> **Read:** A 20% premium is supported by only ~1 in 5. A **10-15% premium** is
> the realistic ceiling for the mass of the segment.

### 4. Most Requested Features
| Rank | Feature | % |
|------|---------|---|
| 1 | **QR code to ingredient sourcing** | **44%** (22/50) |
| 2 | Refillable packaging | 20% (10/50) |
| 3 | Fragrance-free options | 18% (9/50) |
| 3 | Auto-refill subscription | 18% (9/50) |

### 5. Key Verbatim Quotes
- *"I never know if 'clean' actually means anything. Show me where each ingredient comes from."*
- *"If I could scan a bottle and see the real sourcing, I'd switch today."*
- *"Carbon-neutral shipping is nice but honestly I assume everyone does it now."*
- *"Greenwashing is exhausting. Transparency would earn my trust and my money."*

### 6. "So What?" — 3 Actionable Insights
1. **Reposition around transparency, not shipping.**
   *Evidence:* 72% rank transparency #1; only 28% care about shipping.
   *Next step:* make QR ingredient traceability the hero of the MVP + landing page.
2. **Price the premium at 10-15%, not 20%.**
   *Evidence:* 60% accept 10-15%; only 22% accept 20%.
   *Next step:* model unit economics at a 12% premium.
3. **Build QR traceability into v1, not v2.**
   *Evidence:* it's the single most-requested feature (44%) *and* a competitive gap.
   *Next step:* scope a minimal per-ingredient QR page for the first 2 SKUs.
